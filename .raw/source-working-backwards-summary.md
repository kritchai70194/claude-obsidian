# Source, Working Backwards (Bryar and Carr) summary

## PR/FAQ Process

> "Before we start building, we write a Press Release to clearly define how the new idea or product will benefit customers, and we create a list of Frequently Asked Questions to resolve the tough issues up front."

Structure:
- **Press Release.** How the new product or idea benefits customers, in market-ready language. Typically less than one page.
- **FAQ section.** Addresses tough issues and unresolved questions upfront. Usually 5 pages or fewer.

The process emphasizes "seeking truth" through relentless questioning of assumptions *before* development begins. If the press release isn't exciting, the product isn't worth building.

## Two-Pizza Teams

> "Teams sized such that the teams would be no larger than the number of people that could be adequately fed by two large pizzas."

Rationale: maintains agility, reduces coordination overhead, preserves autonomous decision-making capability. Practical implication: roughly 6 to 10 people.

## Single-Threaded Leadership (STL)

> "A single person, unencumbered by competing responsibilities, owns a single major initiative and heads up a separable, largely autonomous team to deliver its goals."

Purpose: enables focus and accountability without distraction from competing projects. Common Amazon failure mode this addresses: a leader who owns 3 things owns nothing well.

## Bar Raiser Hiring Process

> "A scalable, repeatable, formal process for consistently making appropriate and successful hiring decisions."

Components:
- STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for structuring interviews
- Bar Raiser role: "Involved in every interview loop" to prevent bias, coach interviewers, and ensure candidates meet or exceed company standards

Effect: "Minimize the variability of ad hoc hiring processes, and improve results."

## Working Backwards from the Customer

Approach product development by defining desired customer experience first, then iteratively working backward to clarify what to build. The PR/FAQ is the artifact that forces this discipline.

## Narrative Over Slides (the 6-pager)

Amazon relies "far more on the written word" than most companies. The six-page narrative document replaces PowerPoint in leadership meetings. Discussions begin only after silent reading of the full narrative document, typically the first 20 minutes of the meeting.

Reason articulated by Bezos in 2004 ban-on-PowerPoint memo: bullet-pointed slides hide the holes that narrative prose forces you to surface.

## Input vs Output Metrics

Focus on "controllable input metrics" (selection, price, convenience) rather than output metrics (revenue, share price) that cannot be directly manipulated.

The principle: optimize for what you can act on; revenue is downstream of inputs and therefore a lagging signal at best.

## Weekly Business Review (WBR)

Fractal review structure enabling continuous weekly improvement across teams and business units. Same shape at team, group, and org level. Same metrics framework. Differences in scope.

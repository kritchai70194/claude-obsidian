# Carmack Unscripted: Meta Connect 2022 Talk + Carmack/Bosworth Twitter Spaces

Two combined sources from the Meta Connect 2022 event window (October 2022), both featuring Carmack speaking unscripted about the state of VR and his coming AGI focus.

Sources:
- Meta Connect 2022 Carmack Q&A transcript: https://www.alwaysleaveanote.com/2023/01/transcript-of-meta-connect-2022-main.html
- YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ouq5yyzSiAw
- Carmack/Bosworth Twitter Spaces: https://www.uploadvr.com/carmack-bosworth-recording-twitter/

## Themes

This was the last major Carmack-at-Meta public talk before the December 2022 resignation. Given in VR (first time he did an unscripted VR talk inside a virtual environment). Tone: candid, technical, exasperated, hopeful for AGI.

## Verbatim quotes

### On the killer VR product
"If we took the specifications of what Quest 2 does today, and we made clearer optics, better ergonomics, longer battery life and cheaper... that would be the killer product."

### On Quest 2 market position
"Quest 2 is doing really damn well."

"A bunch of developers are really doing pretty damn well on Quest. And we're expecting that to improve."

### On VR as a productivity device
"We need to be something that somebody hard up for money decides I'm going to buy a VR headset instead of a Chromebook."

"We need to have similar app libraries. We need to be just as effective with keyboard and mouse."

### On the Metaverse
"I really fought to keep us from attempting to do the metaverse because I just expected it to be a disaster."

### On controller-free VR
"We want to be able to have a controller-free SKU in the future where we rely just on hand tracking."

### On Instant VR (friction reduction)
"It should also be instant. You should just be able to like pull it up and put it on your head to just glance over there."

"What would your phone be like if you had to set up and acknowledge guardian every time, if you had to wait for your controllers to wake up before you could start doing inputs there?"

### On VR's positioning challenge
"VR needs to displace all of these other devices. It needs to replace those things."

"They can be cheaper than cell phones in some ways. It's just a matter of doing it... we just need to go do it."

"It's Meta's to lose at this point."

### On VR motion sickness (system-level solution)
On depth-aware vignetting: he said it is "an objectively calculatable thing" that could work globally across experiences, and should be solved at the OS layer not per-app.

"It'll probably take us two years to sort of work something out and push it through developers."

### On AGI timeline (the famous Spaces quote)
"By 2030, there's a 50/50 chance that someone in the industry, and maybe it's me, has line of sight... at Artificial General Intelligence."

### On his AGI larval phase
"I've left my larval phase... where I'm kind of cocooned up learning about all the basic things."

### Engineering-vs-organization commentary
On Meta's development friction: deployment processes are painful, multitasking latency in VR remains ~500ms, browser integration requires deep OS-level integration not yet achieved.

"The programmers just need to do this instead of that. All of that's just right there for us."

### On VR design philosophy
Successful titles prioritize replayability through "intrinsically fulfilling" actions (weapon feedback, movement feel) over narrative progression. Hook players in the first 10-15 minutes rather than complex mechanics.

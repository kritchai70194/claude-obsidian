## Core Principles

**Manager-to-Engineer Ratio:** "Managers should support 6-8 engineers," enabling time for coaching, strategy development, and organizational leadership.

**Manager-to-Manager Ratio:** "Managers should support 4-6 managers" to allow adequate coaching, stakeholder alignment, and organizational investment.

**On-Call Sustainability:** "Oncall rotations want 8 engineers" for effective two-tier 24/7 production support. Larson notes that shared rotations across teams represent only a temporary solution.

## Four Functional Roles

- **Tech Lead Managers (TLMs):** Managers supporting fewer than four engineers typically handle design and implementation work alongside management. Limited career progression.
- **Coaches:** Managers overseeing more than eight or nine engineers function primarily as problem-solvers, unable to actively invest in their teams or domains.
- **Ramping Up:** Managers supporting fewer than four other managers should be in active learning phases, not steady state.

## The Playbook

- Teams should maintain 6-8 members during stable operations.
- New teams emerge by growing existing teams to 8-10, then splitting into two groups of 4-5.
- Never establish empty teams or allow managers to exceed eight direct reports.

## Critical Principle

"Small teams (<4) are not teams." Larson states he has "regretted it every single time" when sponsoring 1-2 person teams, emphasizing that smaller units function as individuals rather than true teams.

## Tech Lead

The most common Staff archetype. Tech Leads guide one team or a cluster of teams in their approach and execution.

- Scope complex tasks and coordinate teams toward solutions.
- Maintain team context and cross-team relationships.
- Partner closely with product managers.
- Define technical vision and build alignment on complex issues.
- Delegate implementation work to grow teammates.

Time allocation: reduced coding; increased coordination and vision-setting work.

Quote: "They're comfortable scoping complex tasks, coordinating their team towards solving them, and unblocking them along the way."

## Architect

Architects own the direction, quality, and approach within critical technical domains that are both complex and central to company success.

- Maintain intimate understanding of business needs, user goals, and technical constraints.
- Identify and advocate for effective approaches within their domain.
- Operate with earned organizational authority based on demonstrated judgment.
- May or may not write code depending on company philosophy.

Quote: "Architects are responsible for the success of a specific technical domain within their company."

## Solver

Trusted organizational agents who go deep into complex, high-risk problems identified as critical priorities by leadership.

- Work on problems with unclear approaches or high execution risk.
- Continue until problems are resolved or contained.
- Operate with minimal organizational politics (problems already prioritized).
- Handle business-technology intersection issues.

Quote: "The Solver is a trusted agent of the organization who goes deep into knotty problems, continuing to work on them until they're resolved."

## Right Hand

The rarest archetype, existing only in large organizations (hundreds of engineers). Operates as a senior leader without direct reports, borrowing authority from an executive.

- Scale executive's impact by removing important problems from their plate.
- Address issues at the intersection of business, technology, people, culture, and process.
- Attend leadership staff meetings.
- Identify fires, edit approach, delegate execution.

Quote: "The Right Hand extends an executive's attention, borrowing their scope and authority to operate particularly complex organizations."

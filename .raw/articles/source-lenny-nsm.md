# Source, Lenny Choosing Your North Star Metric

## What This Is

Lenny's canonical essay on choosing a North Star Metric (NSM). Based on a survey of current and past employees at 40+ growth-stage companies. The most-cited NSM framework in modern product practice.

## Definition

> "The top-line metrics that all company priorities are aligned around."

A single metric. Not a dashboard. The company's strategy compressed into one number.

## The Central Thesis

> "Your North Star Metric is your strategy, and your strategy is your North Star Metric."

The reciprocal claim. The NSM is not a measurement tool; it is the strategy. If you cannot articulate the strategy by pointing to one metric, you do not yet have a strategy.

## The Six Types of NSM

From the 40+ company survey, Lenny identified six categories:

1. **Revenue** — "The amount of money being generated." ~50% of companies use this. Lenny explicitly cautions against it (see Common Mistakes).
2. **Customer Growth** — "The number of users who are paying." ~35% of companies.
3. **Consumption Growth** — "The intensity of usage of your product, beyond simply visiting your site."
4. **Engagement Growth** — "The number of users who are simply active in your product." ~30% (overlaps with above).
5. **Growth Efficiency** — "The efficiency at which you spend vs. make money." ~10%.
6. **User Experience** — "The measure of how enjoyable and easy to use customers find the product."

## Company Examples by Business Model

- **Marketplaces/Platforms:** Airbnb (nights booked), Uber, Lyft, Twilio, Plaid
- **Paid-Growth:** Blue Apron, Casper, Hims, Calm
- **Freemium B2B:** Coda, Slack, Airtable, Asana, Dropbox
- **UGC Subscription:** Twitch, Loom
- **Ad-Driven:** Facebook, Pinterest, Snap, Spotify
- **Consumer Subscription:** Duolingo, Tinder, Strava, Patreon

The business model strongly predicts the right NSM category. Marketplaces lean toward consumption metrics; ad-driven leans toward engagement; B2B leans toward customer growth or growth efficiency.

## Jobs-to-Be-Done Connection

The NSM should answer: "What jobs are our users hiring our product to do?"

Examples:
- Plaid: "link my bank account" → NSM "bank accounts linked"
- Lyft: "get a quick ride" → NSM "number of rides"

The JTBD frame ensures the NSM is customer-centered, not company-centered. A metric like "monthly active users" is company-centered (we want users to be active). A metric like "rides completed" is customer-centered (the customer's job is to get from A to B).

## Common Mistakes

### Revenue as NSM (the most common error)

> Revenue focus risks:
> - "It's spiky, and thus hard to make operational"
> - "Can lead to suboptimal decisions, such as spending too much time optimizing pricing"
> - "Can be uninspiring to the team"

Half of companies use revenue, but Lenny explicitly recommends against it for most. Revenue is the OUTPUT; the NSM should be the leading INPUT that drives revenue.

### Vanity metrics

Metrics that go up regardless of whether customers are getting value. Total registered users; total downloads; total pageviews. These can rise even as the product fails.

### Composite metrics

Made-up scores ("Engagement Index" = .3*DAU + .2*sessions + ...). They are not intuitive, not actionable, and not honest. Pick one underlying number.

## The Selection Framework

> "Which metric, if it were to increase today, would most accelerate my business' flywheel?"

The diagnostic question. The answer is your NSM candidate. Then:

1. Identify the input metrics that drive the NSM
2. Focus team activities on those inputs
3. Tie company OKRs to the NSM
4. Communicate the NSM externally (employees, board, sometimes users)

## What This Source Teaches

- The NSM is the strategy compressed into one number.
- Six categories cover most companies; business model predicts category.
- Jobs-to-be-Done framing keeps the NSM customer-centered.
- Revenue is the most common NSM and usually wrong; it is an output, not a driver.
- Composite metrics are dishonest; pick one underlying number.
- The selection question: which single metric, increased today, most accelerates the flywheel?

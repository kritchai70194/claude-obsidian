# Bringing Depression Out of The Shadows In Startups

**Source:** feld.com
**URL:** https://feld.com/archives/2015/04/bringing-depression-shadows-startups/
**Date:** 2015-04-08
**Author:** Brad Feld
**Format:** Q&A reposted from Denver Business Journal

## Summary

A Q&A in which Feld speaks to the prevalence of depression among founders and his own three discrete depressive episodes.

## Episodes named

- **Mid-20s** — first clinical episode, while running a successful company. Did not talk about it because he was afraid people would stop taking him seriously or respecting him.
- **2001 post-9/11** — second episode, triggered partly by being in NYC the morning of 9/11. Terrified, exhausted, emotionally distressed.
- **Late 2012 into 2013** — third episode, triggered by accumulated physical exhaustion, a bike accident, intensive writing projects, pet loss, and medical emergencies. This is the episode he wrote about publicly in 2013.

## Frames

- Depression in startup circles is "very common, although it is rarely discussed."
- Stress, anxiety, and depression often blur together in founders; most experience some mental-health difficulty at some point.
- "Leaders and entrepreneurs are programmed to 'never show weakness.'" This programming is the obstacle, not the depression itself.
- Triggers Feld names: physiological exhaustion, lack of learning opportunities, accumulation of small physical and emotional injuries.

## Recommendations

- Talk to mentors and peers.
- Practice vulnerability.
- Use organizations like Jerry Colonna's Reboot.io for structured peer support.

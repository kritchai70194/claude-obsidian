# VC Rights: Up, Down, And Know What The Fuck Is Going On

**Source:** feld.com
**URL:** https://feld.com/archives/2012/05/vc-rights-up-down-and-know-what-the-fuck-is-going-on/
**Date:** 2012-05-07
**Author:** Brad Feld

## Summary

Feld recounts a story from an HBS VC Alumni event where a veteran VC distilled the entire term-sheet negotiation down to three rights a VC actually needs:

1. **Up** — pro-rata rights. When the company is doing well, the VC wants the right to continue investing to maintain ownership.
2. **Down** — liquidation preference. When the company is doing poorly, the VC wants to get money out first.
3. **Know What The Fuck Is Going On** — a board seat (or equivalent observer/information rights). Visibility into actual operations.

Feld attributes the framing through Fred Wilson to Wilson's mentor Milt Pappas, signaling broad consensus among experienced investors.

## Load-bearing claim

Term sheets are long and full of jargon, but the substantive rights reduce to these three. Everything else is noise, lawyer-driven complexity, or pathology. A clean term sheet is one that gives the VC these three and not much more.

## Implication

If an investor is asking for substantially more than Up/Down/KWTFIGO, they are planning for an adversarial relationship. Read the deal terms as a forecast of the investor's behavior post-close.

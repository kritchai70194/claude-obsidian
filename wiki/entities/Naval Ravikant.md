# Naval Ravikant

Founder and former CEO of AngelList (2010-2024), the platform that rebuilt how early-stage capital flows. Co-founder of Epinions (1999, later Shopping.com). Angel investor in 200+ startups including Uber, Twitter, Postmates, Notion, Yammer, OpenDNS. Author of the "How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)" tweetstorm (May 2018), reframed into book form by Eric Jorgenson as *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* (2020), freely available at navalmanack.com.

Anchor mentor on the **wealth-creation and leverage layer** of the founder-resilience pole, complementary to [[Ben Horowitz]] (wartime operating), [[Brad Feld]] (mental health and rhythm), [[Brian Chesky]] (founder mode at scale). Where Horowitz tells you how to keep operating when everything is on fire, Naval tells you what to compound across decades so the fire happens less often.

---

## Why This Mentor

Naval fills the unfair-advantage and wealth-creation layer that no other mentor in the brain explicitly names. [[Paul Graham]] writes about startup mechanics. [[Hamilton Helmer]] names structural powers. [[Andrew Chen]] writes about growth loops. None of them ask the prior question: what should I be compounding in myself, across my whole career, that makes any of those possible.

Naval's signature claim: the durable returns in life (wealth, knowledge, relationships, reputation) all come from compound interest on rare, hard-to-replicate inputs. **Specific knowledge** (uncopyable, unteachable skill), multiplied by **leverage** (code, media, capital, labor), under **judgment** (being right under uncertainty), played as a **long-term game with long-term people**. Every one of those terms is precise and operational, not motivational.

For a technical CEO, Naval's frames answer the question Horowitz does not: what am I actually building in myself over the next 20 years, not just this quarter. The four sources below give us the tweetstorm (the compressed canon), the book (the expanded synthesis), the long-form interview (the lived application), and the single most operational concept (specific knowledge) in one place.

## Core Frameworks

**Specific Knowledge.** Knowledge you cannot get by going to school. If it could be trained, it would already be commoditized. Found by pursuing genuine curiosity in a way that feels like play to you but looks like work to others. Examples: writing, design taste, judgment under uncertainty, sales ability, a specific technical idiom. Society does not value it until you become irreplaceable at it.

**Leverage.** The multiplier on specific knowledge. Four kinds:
1. **Labor** (people working for you). Oldest form. Requires management.
2. **Capital** (money working for you). Requires judgment. Limited to those who already have it.
3. **Code** (software working for you). Permissionless. Scales infinitely. Zero marginal cost.
4. **Media** (content working for you). Permissionless. Audiences compound.

Code and media are the new permissionless leverage. You do not need anyone's approval to start.

**Long-term games with long-term people.** Compound interest applies to relationships, knowledge, and reputation just as much as to capital. The same people in repeated games produce disproportionate returns. Burn no bridges. Pay forward. Play games where the payoff is multi-decade.

**Wealth, Money, Status.** Three different things, regularly confused.
- **Wealth** is owning the means of producing while you sleep (businesses, equity, real estate, royalties).
- **Money** is how we transfer wealth, a unit of account.
- **Status** is your rank in the social hierarchy.
Status games are zero-sum and addictive. Wealth games are positive-sum. Money is just the in-between.

**Judgment.** Rare and well-rewarded. Hard to acquire because you must be right under uncertainty, and most situations do not give you clean feedback loops. Built by reading, reflecting, taking accountability, and surviving enough cycles to develop pattern recognition.

## Primary-Source Citations

### From [[Naval How to Get Rich]] (2018 tweetstorm)

The compressed canon. 40 tweets posted May 31 2018, since reproduced verbatim across the web. The single most operational text on individual wealth creation in the post-internet era.

> Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep.

> You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale.

> Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. You can create software and media that works for you while you sleep.

### From [[Naval Almanack]] (Eric Jorgenson, 2020)

The expanded synthesis. Two parts: Wealth and Happiness. Chapters cover seek wealth not money or status, find and build specific knowledge, play long-term games with long-term people, take on accountability, build or buy equity, find a position of leverage, prioritize and focus, find work that feels like play, build mental models, learn to love reading.

> The most important skill for getting rich is becoming a perpetual learner.

> Earn with your mind, not your time.

### From [[Naval Joe Rogan JRE 1309]] (June 2019)

The lived application. Two hours on happiness as a skill, desire vs contentment, the role of meditation, why most ambition is unfulfilled, the angel investing playbook, how leverage compounds across decades.

Theme: ambition is a contract you sign with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want. Most people sign it without realizing it. Happiness is the skill of unsigning it without losing the work ethic.

### From [[Naval Specific Knowledge]] (synthesis across sources)

The single most operationally useful Naval concept. The only durable edge in an efficient market is something the market cannot teach itself. Pursued through genuine curiosity, not credential-chasing. Recognizable because it feels like play to you and looks like work to everyone else.

## Core Frames (now backed by citations)

- **Seek wealth, not money or status.** ([[Naval How to Get Rich]], [[Naval Almanack]]) Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep. Status is zero-sum. Money is just the transfer mechanism.
- **Specific knowledge cannot be trained, only attracted.** ([[Naval Specific Knowledge]], [[Naval Almanack]]) If it could be taught, it would already be commoditized. Find it by following genuine curiosity.
- **Leverage is the multiplier; specific knowledge is the input.** ([[Naval How to Get Rich]], [[Naval Almanack]]) Four kinds: code, media, capital, labor. Code and media are permissionless.
- **Play long-term games with long-term people.** ([[Naval Almanack]], [[Naval Joe Rogan JRE 1309]]) Compound interest applies to relationships, knowledge, and reputation, not just capital.
- **All returns in life come from compound interest.** ([[Naval Almanack]]) Wealth, knowledge, fitness, relationships. The unit varies; the mechanic does not.
- **Earn with your mind, not your time.** ([[Naval Almanack]], [[Naval How to Get Rich]]) Hourly wages cap at biology. Equity and ownership do not.
- **Judgment is rare and well-rewarded.** ([[Naval Almanack]]) Hard to acquire because you must be right under uncertainty.
- **Take risks under your own name.** ([[Naval How to Get Rich]], [[Naval Almanack]]) The downside is blame; the upside is unbounded. Most people choose the salary because they cannot bear the named downside.
- **Work like a lion, not a cow.** ([[Naval How to Get Rich]]) Sprint hard at the right targets, then rest. The 9-to-5 is a relic of factory work.
- **Happiness is a skill, not a circumstance.** ([[Naval Joe Rogan JRE 1309]], [[Naval Almanack]]) Built through reducing desire, not increasing acquisition.

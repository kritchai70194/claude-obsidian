# Mark Roberge

Founding Chief Revenue Officer at HubSpot from 2007 to 2013, where he grew revenue from $0 to $100M ARR and scaled the sales team from 1 to 450. Senior Lecturer at Harvard Business School since 2014, teaching the entrepreneurial sales course. Managing Director at Stage 2 Capital, the first VC firm staffed and backed by go-to-market operators. Author of *The Sales Acceleration Formula* (Wiley, 2015), the canonical text on scaling B2B sales from zero using metrics, hiring rubrics, and process discipline. MIT engineering undergrad, MIT Sloan MBA. The defining voice on data-driven sales operations.

Anchor mentor on the **GTM/Sales pole**, complementary to [[Aaron Ross]] (outbound prospecting and the SDR motion's origin), [[April Dunford]] (positioning as strategic discipline upstream of sales motion), and [[Andy Raskin]] (strategic narrative as the frame that makes a sales pitch land). Where Ross invented the SDR/AE machine, Dunford defines what you sell, and Raskin defines how you frame it, Roberge defines how you measure, hire, and operate the engine.

## Why This Mentor

The existing brain assumed product sells itself: founder-led sales, [[PG Do Things That Dont Scale]], [[Chen Growth Loops]] for product-led growth. Roberge fills the gap that opens the day founder-led sales stops scaling and the company needs a repeatable sales motion that runs without the founder in every deal.

His HubSpot run is the foundational case study. He joined as employee #4 and stayed seven years, building the sales organization from a single rep (himself) to 450 across multiple geographies. The growth was not luck. He treated sales as an engineering discipline: define inputs (hires, leads, training), measure outputs (conversion rates, ramp time, quota attainment), iterate the formula until the system runs predictably.

Three contributions matter most. First, **scientific sales hiring**: stop hiring on gut feel, define the traits that predict performance in your specific motion, build a rubric, score every interview, validate which traits actually correlate with quota attainment six months later. Second, the **four-step formula** (hire the same way, train the same way, manage the same way, generate demand the same way), which makes sales scalable because it makes sales reproducible. Third, **buyer-journey-first process design**: the sales process is not the pipeline stages your CRM tracks; it is the decision stages your buyer moves through. Mismatch kills conversion.

Frames here: predictability beats heroics; the salesperson who hits 200% of quota is a recruiting problem, not a celebration, because you cannot hire 50 more of them; CAC and LTV are not vanity metrics, they are the only metrics; if your unit economics break at scale, fix the product or the price, not the sales motion.

## Key Frameworks

**The Sales Acceleration Formula (4 steps).** Hire the same kind of salesperson, repeatedly. Train them the same way, every cohort. Manage them with the same process, every deal. Generate demand predictably, every month. Each step is a function with inputs you control and outputs you measure. If sales is unpredictable, one of the four is broken.

**Scientific Sales Hiring.** Define the salesperson profile that wins in your specific motion. Roberge's five HubSpot traits: coachability, curiosity, prior success, work ethic, intelligence. Score every interview against the rubric. Track which traits correlate with quota attainment 12 months in. Update the rubric annually. The output is a hiring machine that produces predictable rep performance, not a roulette wheel.

**The SDR/AE Split.** Sales Development Reps prospect, qualify, and book meetings. Account Executives close. The split exists because the skill sets diverge: prospecting rewards volume, resilience, and disciplined cadence; closing rewards listening, deal navigation, and commercial judgment. Generalists do both badly. Specialization is how sales orgs scale past the first 10 reps.

**Lead-to-Revenue Funnel.** Lead → MQL (marketing qualified) → SQL (sales qualified) → opportunity → closed-won. Measure the conversion rate at every stage. Coach to the bottleneck. If MQL-to-SQL is the choke point, the issue is qualification criteria or SDR skill. If SQL-to-opportunity is the choke point, the issue is AE discovery. Diagnostic precision is the entire point.

**Demand Generation as Math.** CAC payback under 12 months. LTV/CAC at 3:1 or better at scale. Gross Revenue Retention above 90%. Net Revenue Retention above 110%. If the math does not work, no sales playbook fixes it; the problem is upstream in product or pricing.

**Buyer Journey ≠ Seller Journey.** The buyer moves through problem awareness → solution exploration → vendor selection → commercial close. Most sales processes are built around the seller's pipeline stages (discovery call, demo, proposal, negotiation). Mismatch kills conversion because reps are pushing while buyers are not yet ready. Map the sales process to the buyer's decision stages, not the CRM's pipeline columns.

## Core Frames

- **Predictability is the goal; heroics are the failure mode.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]]) A team of 50 reps cannot run on heroics; it runs on systems.
- **Hire scientifically. Score every interview against a rubric. Validate the rubric against 12-month quota attainment.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]])
- **Train every cohort identically. The training is the asset, not the trainer.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]], [[Roberge HubSpot Playbook]])
- **Specialize the org: SDRs prospect, AEs close.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]]) Generalist reps do not scale past the first ten.
- **Measure conversion at every funnel stage. Coach to the bottleneck.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]])
- **CAC payback under 12 months. LTV/CAC over 3:1. NRR over 110%.** ([[Roberge Math of B2B Sales]]) If the math breaks at scale, fix product or pricing, not the sales motion.
- **Map process to the buyer's decision stages, not the seller's pipeline.** ([[Roberge Sales Acceleration Formula]])
- **A 200%-of-quota rep is a recruiting problem, not a hero.** ([[Roberge Interviews Synthesis]]) You cannot hire 50 more of them; reverse-engineer what made them work and bake it into the rubric.
- **The playbook trap: rigid scripts kill creative selling at the top of the market.** ([[Roberge Interviews Synthesis]]) Process discipline at scale; flexibility in the strategic deals.
- **Demand generation is a system, not a campaign.** ([[Roberge HubSpot Playbook]]) Inbound marketing as the perpetual lead-machine; sales consumes leads at a measured rate.

# Andy Jassy

Stub. Andy Jassy is the second CEO of Amazon, succeeding [[Jeff Bezos]] on 2021-07-05. He founded AWS in 2003 and ran it through 2021 before assuming the parent-company role.

Not currently a mentor in the [[Persona]] mentor bench. Filed as a developing entity because:

1. The 2021 shareholder letter ([[Bezos 2021 Letter]] on disk, but Jassy-authored) is the first non-Bezos Amazon shareholder letter and warrants the disambiguation.
2. AWS is the operational artifact of the [[Werner Vogels]] / [[Bezos 2002 API Mandate]] / [[Vogels Distributed Computing Manifesto]] lineage, and Jassy ran it through its formative two decades.
3. Future ingest of Jassy-era Amazon letters (2022, 2023, 2024, 2025) would mature this entity into a primary source.

## Role at Amazon

- **2003-2021**: Founded AWS as a small internal project ("just two pizzas could feed the team"), grew it to ~$60B+ annualized revenue at handoff. The Bezos-era Amazon shareholder letters repeatedly cite AWS as the most important non-retail business; Jassy is the operational executor of that.
- **2021-present**: CEO Amazon. The 2021 shareholder letter ([[Bezos 2021 Letter]] by naming convention, Jassy-authored in content) is his first as parent-company CEO. Successor transition cited in [[Bezos 2021 Letter]].

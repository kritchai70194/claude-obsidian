# Sam Altman

CEO of OpenAI from 2019 through the present, briefly ousted by the board on Nov 17 2023 and reinstated five days later on Nov 22 2023 after a near-total employee revolt. Former President of Y Combinator (2014-2019), where he scaled the accelerator from a few dozen batches a year into the dominant seed-stage institution in tech. Co-founder of Loopt (2005, acquired by Green Dot in 2012). Co-founder of Hydrazine Capital. Stanford CS dropout (2003-2005).

Anchor mentor on the **AI-Native pole**, alongside [[Dario Amodei]] (safety-first lab-builder) and [[Demis Hassabis]] (research-first scientist-CEO). Altman occupies the company-builder seat. Where Amodei argues from safety and Hassabis argues from science, Altman argues from product, capital, and the macroeconomics of intelligence itself.

Polarizing figure. Deeply respected for vision, capital orchestration, and the OpenAI execution arc from 2019 GPT-2 caution through ChatGPT-driven category creation. Questioned for board management, the structure of personal investments, and the gap between OpenAI's stated mission and its competitive posture.

---

## Why This Mentor

The other AI-native mentors describe AI as a research program or a safety problem. Altman describes AI as the next general-purpose technology and reasons backward from that claim about company strategy, capital structure, and policy. He is the canonical operator voice for the question every 2026+ founder faces: *if intelligence becomes a near-zero-cost commodity within a decade, what company are you actually building?*

Two careers stacked. The YC presidency 2014-2019 produced the playbook (ambition, movement vs business, hard problems attract good people, idea matters more than people think) that shaped a decade of seed-stage operating habits. The OpenAI run 2019-present produced the AGI economics thesis (Moore's Law for everything, the Intelligence Age, compute as the new oil) and the operating template for the foundation-model era.

Three contributions matter most. First, the **AGI economics thesis**, which says intelligence cost collapses to roughly zero and policy must redistribute the resulting surplus or society fractures. Second, the **YC playbook**, which named ambition and worldview as structural requirements, not soft virtues. Third, the **OpenAI structural experiment**, the non-profit board governing a capped-profit subsidiary, whose Nov 2023 stress test exposed governance gaps that every AI company now has to think through.

## Key Frameworks

**Moore's Law for Everything.** AI reduces the marginal cost of intelligence toward zero. Every industry that runs on knowledge work, which is most of them, will see its cost structure rewritten. The productivity surplus is enormous, and concentrated. Without redistribution policy, the gains pool with capital and the social contract breaks. Source: [[Altman AGI Economics]].

**The Intelligence Age.** AI is the next general-purpose platform, comparable to electricity or the internet but compressed in time. Productivity gains compound for decades. The next generation will look back at the pre-AI era the way we look back at the pre-electricity era. Source: [[Altman AGI Economics]].

**Compute is the new oil.** Foundation model training and inference require unprecedented capital outlays. $1B training runs by 2025, $10B+ by 2027, plausibly $100B+ by 2030. Companies that own compute (or compute relationships) have structural advantages no software-era playbook predicted. Source: [[Altman Interviews Synthesis]].

**The product is the model plus the workflow.** Not just the model. The wrapper vs platform vs workflow debate misses that the durable artifact is the integrated experience built around the model's capability, including the data feedback loop the workflow generates. Source: [[Altman Interviews Synthesis]].

**Have a high level of ambition and a strong worldview.** The two repeated YC-era counsels. Ambition because the founders who change things aim higher than the median advisor recommends. Worldview because being right about something nobody else is right about is the structural requirement for an outlier outcome. The Graham frame ("do things that don't scale") applied to AGI: the right early metric is users who depend on you, not revenue. Source: [[Altman YC Essays]].

**AGI deployment is its own problem.** Safety has two halves. The research half (alignment, evaluation, capability bounding) is well-discussed. The deployment half (how do we release capability into a society without destabilizing it) is underappreciated and probably harder. Source: [[Altman Interviews Synthesis]].

**The OpenAI structure was an experiment, and the Nov 2023 ousting was the stress test.** Non-profit board, for-profit subsidiary, capped-profit returns to investors, mission as constitutional commitment. The structure is novel and untested. The November 2023 board crisis exposed how thinly defined the governance edges were and how quickly the structure can collapse under capital pressure. Source: [[Altman Board Ousting]].

## Primary-Source Citations

### From [[Altman YC Essays]] (2015 + 2019)

The pre-OpenAI playbook. Cited counsel: focus on a great idea, build a great team, build a great product, get to ramen profitability, then think about growth. Have a strong worldview. Have an extremely high level of ambition. Hire slowly, fire quickly. Movement, not business.

> The best companies are almost always mission-oriented.

> You want to make something that a small number of users love, not something that a large number of users like.

### From [[Altman AGI Economics]] (2021 + 2024)

The canonical AGI-economics essays. Moore's Law for Everything (2021) makes the cost-collapse claim. The Intelligence Age (Sept 2024) makes the timeline claim and stakes a position on the productivity dividend.

> In the next couple of decades, we will be able to do things that would have seemed like magic to our grandparents.

> Many of the jobs we do today would have looked like trifling wastes of time to people a few hundred years ago, but nobody is looking back at the past, wishing they were a lamplighter.

### From [[Altman Interviews Synthesis]]

Recurring themes across Lex Fridman (episodes 367+), Trevor Noah What Now, Bill Gates Unconfuse Me, and Davos appearances. Timelines (superintelligence plausibly by ~2030), mission framing (AGI for all of humanity), the Microsoft relationship (capital + distribution + independence), the safety-vs-capability balance (both have to advance together).

### From [[Altman Board Ousting]]

The Nov 17-22 2023 governance crisis. Board's stated rationale ("not consistently candid"). Employee response: more than 700 of 770 staff threatened to resign. Microsoft offered to hire the entire team. Reinstated Nov 22. The lessons: founder-CEO + board governance in fast-moving AI companies is structurally fragile; the OpenAI capped-profit structure had governance gaps; the capital base (employees, Microsoft) had become the de facto board.

## Core Frames

- **AI is the next general-purpose technology, on the scale of electricity or the internet.** ([[Altman AGI Economics]]) Companies built without this assumption are mispricing the future.
- **Moore's Law for everything: the cost of intelligence collapses to near zero, the productivity dividend is enormous, redistribution is required.** ([[Altman AGI Economics]]) Policy frame as much as company frame.
- **Compute is the new oil.** ([[Altman Interviews Synthesis]]) Capital intensity for foundation models is unlike any prior software era.
- **The product is the model plus the workflow built around it.** ([[Altman Interviews Synthesis]]) The model alone is rarely defensible.
- **Have a very high level of ambition. Have a strong worldview.** ([[Altman YC Essays]]) The two repeated counsels to founders.
- **Mission-orientation correlates with outlier outcomes.** ([[Altman YC Essays]]) Movement before business.
- **Make something a small number of users love.** ([[Altman YC Essays]]) Early metric is depth of dependence, not breadth of audience.
- **AGI deployment is its own discipline, separate from alignment research.** ([[Altman Interviews Synthesis]]) The hard problem is releasing capability into the world without destabilizing it.
- **Governance structures for AI labs are unsettled.** ([[Altman Board Ousting]]) The OpenAI capped-profit structure was novel, the Nov 2023 ousting exposed its governance gaps, every AI-native founder now has to think harder about board composition.

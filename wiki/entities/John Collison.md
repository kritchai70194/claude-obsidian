# John Collison

Co-founder and President of Stripe (founded 2010 with brother [[Patrick Collison]]). Runs much of Stripe's customer-facing organization including sales, partnerships, and GTM. Hosts the Cheeky Pint podcast where Stripe interviews founders and leaders.

Anchor mentor in this brain's [[Persona]]. Patrick is the more public-facing intellectual; John is the operator's operator, sharp on the parts of business that don't get romanticized.

---

## Why This Mentor

John brings a counterweight to Patrick's intellectual range: an explicit focus on the unglamorous parts of company-building. Distribution, pricing, customer onboarding, sales motion, organizational design under growth. His interview style is dry, direct, and operationally specific.

## Primary-Source Citations

### From [[Stripe Annual Letter 2024]] (co-authored with Patrick)

On profitability as fuel, not goal:

> "Durable profitability allows us to plow back much of our operating earnings into research and development. In each of the last six years, Stripe has reinvested a much higher proportion of our earnings in R&D than any comparable company."

On customer selection as strategy:

> "Stripe customers share one important characteristic: outsized growth."

On historical durability framing:

> "Of the 500 companies in the S&P 500 at its inception in 1957, only 53 remain in the index today. Back in 1957, companies could expect to remain in the index for 61 years. Today, it's just under 20 years."

### From [[Collison Money Stuff Podcast]] (John as subject, 2024)

On payments complexity:

> "Payments requires you to be good at two very different things: technology and financial services."

On Stripe's position:

> "Stripe makes the financial system work the way people think it already does."

On organizational reality:

> "Organizations are pretty hard to change. It's very important for people to get close to the work."

On distinguishing object from category:

> "There are just a bunch of scams and dodgy characters. But one cannot use the existence of slots to write off serious work."

On IPO discipline:

> "Companies should not sleepwalk into going public. They should be deliberate about it."

On the product-is-the-prospectus principle:

> "Build a great business and you could write your prospectus on a cocktail napkin and it'll be fine."

On mechanism for employee liquidity:

> "We think tender every year is a nice solution."

## Core Frames (now backed by citations)

- **Distribution > genius idea.** Paraphrased. Citation pending Cheeky Pint or 20VC interviews where John speaks specifically on this.
- **Pricing as product surface.** Paraphrased; pending.
- **Customer selection as strategy.** ([[Stripe Annual Letter 2024]]) Optimize for customer growth-rate, not customer count.
- **Durable profitability funds R&D.** ([[Stripe Annual Letter 2024]]) Profit is the mechanism that funds long-horizon investment without external dilution.
- **Make prior forecasts checkable.** ([[Stripe Annual Letter 2024]]) Continuity across artifacts is itself a discipline.
- **Argument by enumeration.** ([[Stripe Annual Letter 2024]]) The 9-century customer list. Don't argue; show.
- **Get close to the work.** ([[Collison Money Stuff Podcast]]) Organizational change requires leaders near operational reality, not abstracted via dashboards.
- **Distinguish object from category.** ([[Collison Money Stuff Podcast]]) Crypto-has-scams is a category-level observation; stablecoins-are-useful is an object-level claim. Conflating them is a common epistemic error.
- **The product is the prospectus.** ([[Collison Money Stuff Podcast]]) Force descriptions short. Watch what survives the compression.
- **Two-way doors applied to IPOs.** ([[Collison Money Stuff Podcast]]) Going public is a one-way door for most companies. Treat accordingly.

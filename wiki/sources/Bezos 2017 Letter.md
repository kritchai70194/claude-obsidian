# Bezos 2017 Letter (the High Standards letter)

[[Jeff Bezos]]'s 2017 shareholder letter. Famously focused on what "high standards" actually means as an operating discipline. Contains the most detailed published account of Amazon's six-page narrative memo practice from Bezos himself (as opposed to the institutional account in [[Working Backwards Book]]).

## Headline Takeaway

Three claims about high standards that are not obvious:

1. **They are teachable.** Not an intrinsic personality trait.
2. **They are domain-specific.** A high standard in writing doesn't transfer to a high standard in hiring.
3. **Scope is part of the standard.** A team producing low-quality memos may have a scope problem, not a skill problem. They think a memo is a one-day task; teach them to budget a week and the memos improve.

## Key Quotes

### On High Standards as Learnable

> "I believe high standards are teachable. In fact, people are pretty good at learning high standards simply through exposure."

### On Domain Specificity

> "I believe high standards are domain specific, and that you have to learn high standards separately in every arena of interest."

### On the Six-Page Memo (Bezos in his own voice, not Bryar/Carr)

> "We don't do PowerPoint (or any other slide-oriented) presentations at Amazon. Instead, we write narratively structured six-page memos. We silently read one at the beginning of each meeting in a kind of 'study hall.'"

### On Memo Quality

> "The great memos are written and re-written, shared with colleagues who are asked to improve the work, set aside for a couple of days, and then edited again with a fresh mind."

### On Scope as a Lever

> "The key point here is that you can improve results through the simple act of teaching scope, that a great memo probably should take a week or more."

### Day 1 Continuity

> "As always, I attach a copy of our original 1997 letter. It remains Day 1."

## What This Brain Takes From It

- **Standards are teachable, not innate.** Important because it changes how you manage talent. Don't filter for people with high standards; teach high standards through exposure to high-standard work.
- **Standards are domain-specific.** Don't transfer a high-standard reputation across domains. Each new domain has to be learned.
- **Scope is part of the standard.** New operating principle. Teaching scope (how long should this take? how much effort should this be?) often unlocks more improvement than teaching technique.
- **The six-page memo defended in Bezos's own voice.** Useful for citing this discipline when [[Working Backwards Book]] (Bryar and Carr) feels secondary.

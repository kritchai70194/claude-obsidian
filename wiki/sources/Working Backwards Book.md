# Working Backwards (Bryar and Carr, 2021)

The canonical insider account of Amazon's operating mechanisms by two long-time Amazon executives (Colin Bryar and Bill Carr, 27 combined years at Amazon). This brain treats the book as the institutional companion to [[Jeff Bezos]]'s shareholder letters: where the letters state principles, this book describes the mechanisms.

## Headline Takeaway

Where the shareholder letters say "we focus on customers and think long-term," this book says: here is the specific six-page memo format, here is the size of the autonomous team, here is the hiring mechanism, here is the metric structure. The mechanisms ARE the culture. Amazon's operating discipline is reproducible because it lives in artifacts (PR/FAQ documents, two-pizza teams, bar raisers, narrative meetings), not vibes.

This unblocks three Operating Principles previously paraphrased: working backwards, two-pizza teams, and the broader "write the memo before the meeting" discipline.

## The Six Mechanisms

### 1. PR/FAQ Process (Working Backwards)

> "Before we start building, we write a Press Release to clearly define how the new idea or product will benefit customers, and we create a list of Frequently Asked Questions to resolve the tough issues up front."

Press release: less than one page, describes the product as if launched, in customer-facing language. FAQ: up to five pages, addresses the hard questions before any code is written. If the press release isn't exciting, the product isn't worth building.

### 2. Two-Pizza Teams

> "Teams sized such that the teams would be no larger than the number of people that could be adequately fed by two large pizzas."

Roughly 6 to 10 people. Limits coordination overhead. Preserves autonomous decision-making. The principle behind the size: communication overhead grows faster than output past a certain team size, so the team size is the lever.

### 3. Single-Threaded Leadership (STL)

> "A single person, unencumbered by competing responsibilities, owns a single major initiative and heads up a separable, largely autonomous team to deliver its goals."

Addresses the failure mode of leaders owning 3 things and owning none well. STL means: one initiative, one leader, one team, one objective. The leader has nothing else competing for their attention.

### 4. Bar Raiser Hiring Process

> "A scalable, repeatable, formal process for consistently making appropriate and successful hiring decisions."

Components:
- STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for structured interviews
- Bar Raiser role: present in every interview loop to prevent bias, coach interviewers, ensure candidates meet or exceed company standards

Result: "Minimize the variability of ad hoc hiring processes, and improve results."

### 5. Narrative Over Slides (the 6-pager)

> Amazon relies "far more on the written word" than most companies.

The six-page narrative document replaces PowerPoint in leadership meetings. Discussions begin only after silent reading, typically the first 20 minutes of the meeting.

Bezos's 2004 ban-on-PowerPoint rationale (per the book): bullet-pointed slides hide the holes that narrative prose forces you to surface. The author cannot get away with handwaving in a paragraph the way they can in a bullet.

### 6. Input vs Output Metrics

Focus on "controllable input metrics" (selection, price, convenience) rather than output metrics (revenue, share price) that cannot be directly manipulated.

The principle: optimize for what you can act on. Revenue is downstream of inputs and therefore a lagging signal at best. Steering by output metrics is steering by the rearview mirror.

### 7. Weekly Business Review (WBR)

Fractal review structure: same shape at team, group, and org levels. Same metrics framework. Different scopes. Continuous weekly improvement instead of quarterly heroics.

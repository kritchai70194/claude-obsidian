# Collison, "Questions" Page

[[Patrick Collison]]'s ongoing list of open intellectual problems he finds interesting. The list excludes Stripe-specific topics by design. The point of the page isn't to provide answers; it's to mark what counts as a serious question to a serious operator.

## Headline Takeaway

What a CEO chooses to wonder about reveals their model of the world more than what they assert about it. Patrick's 23 questions cluster around four themes: (1) progress and stagnation, (2) institutional design, (3) information infrastructure, (4) cultural and civilizational durability.

The questions also encode a discipline: ask big questions in public, leave them open, invite contribution, and don't pretend you have an answer when you don't.

## The 23 Questions, Themed

### Progress and Stagnation

- Why are certain things getting so much more expensive?
- Why do there seem to be more examples of rapidly-completed major projects in the past than the present?
- Why is GDP growth so weirdly constant?
- How can we better understand the dynamics of progress in science?
- What's going on with infrastructure?
- Why don't we build nice neighborhoods any more?

### Institutional Design and Replication

- How do you ensure an adequate replacement rate in systems that have no natural way to die?
- How do we help more experimental cities get started?
- Why are there so many successful startups in Stockholm?
- Why isn't China (yet) producing a lot of top-tier research?

### Information Infrastructure

- Will end-user applications ever be truly programmable? If so, how?
- What's the successor to the book? And how could books be improved?
- What's the successor to the scientific paper and the scientific journal?
- Could there be more good blogs?
- Why are programming environments still so primitive?

### Cultural and Behavioral

- How do people decide to make major life changes?
- Is Bloom's "Two Sigma" phenomenon real? If so, what do we do about it?
- What's the right way to understand and model personality?
- What does religion cause?
- Why is there no canon for life's most important questions?
- Why are so many things so much nicer in Switzerland and Japan?
- What influences when people act in accordance with their self-interest and when they don't?
- Why did climatic variability suddenly decline in the Holocene period?

## What This Brain Takes From It

- **High-agency curiosity.** Patrick's questions are operational, not academic. "How do we help more experimental cities get started?" presumes an answer exists and someone could do something about it.
- **Information infrastructure as a Patrick frame.** Five of the 23 questions are about the systems through which knowledge circulates. This is a tell: Patrick thinks the meta-layer (how ideas reach people) is undervalued compared to the object-layer (the ideas themselves).
- **Honesty about not knowing.** A list of questions, public, unanswered, with no embarrassment. This is itself a discipline most public figures avoid.
- **Replication over invention.** Many questions are "why X works here but not there" framing. Patrick is interested in transferring conditions, not generating novel solutions from scratch.

# Maples Backable Founders

The Floodgate essay and the Tim Ferriss episode #690 (June 2023) in which Maples articulates his five-trait filter for backable founders. Operates as the seed-stage founder-evaluation rubric in the capital-strategy pole. Most-cited single passage in Maples's body of work after "Live in the Future" and "Inflections vs Ideas".

## The Five Traits

Maples names five traits that compose a backable founder. The claim is multiplicative: all five required, not additive. A founder strong on four of five is fundable but not backable. The largest seed returns come only from founders who clear all five.

### 1. Insight

> The founder sees something specific that others miss.

Not a generic industry view. A specific, falsifiable claim about how the world works that can be defended with evidence the rest of the market has not yet processed. Convergent with the "insights vs assumptions" framing in [[Maples Pattern Breakers]] and the "secret manifesto" diagnostic.

Diagnostic: ask the founder to state their insight in one sentence. Vague founders produce industry trends. Backable founders produce specific contrarian beliefs.

### 2. Authenticity

> The founder lives the problem.

The founder is not a strategic outsider who has identified an interesting market; the founder is a participant in the problem who has been forced to think about it because their daily life requires them to. Convergent with [[Maples Live in the Future]]: the authentic founder lives in the future of the problem.

Diagnostic: what is the founder's personal relationship to the problem? Is the founder's life materially different because the problem is unsolved? If the founder could leave the problem behind, are they psychologically capable of doing so?

The Brian Chesky / [[Brian Chesky]] case is the canonical example. Hosting airbeds was not market research; it was rent strategy. See [[Chesky 11-Star Experience]].

### 3. Creativity

> The founder designs solutions that other people would not consider.

Not contrarianism for its own sake. Real design creativity: when the founder is shown a problem, the solutions they propose include possibilities that other smart people in the same room did not generate. The Floodgate diagnostic for creativity in seed pitches: do the founders surprise the room with non-obvious moves?

Example given in the Ferriss interview: the early Twitter founders' decision to limit messages to 140 characters was creative, not analytical. Most founders would have designed for longer-form content because longer-form looked more obviously valuable.

### 4. Persuasiveness

> The founder recruits and raises.

Persuasiveness is not charisma. It is the ability to make other talented people choose to spend their working hours on the founder's mission rather than on alternative employment, and to make capital allocators choose to fund this company rather than alternatives. Both are competitive selections.

The seed test: can the founder recruit at least one engineer or operator who could plausibly be working anywhere? If yes, persuasiveness is verified. If the founder cannot pull a single great hire, the persuasiveness trait is absent regardless of how well the founder pitches.

Maples is explicit: this trait is not about being a smooth talker. Many backable founders are awkward in pitches. The verification is empirical: do great people follow them?

### 5. Stamina

> The founder is in the multi-decade game.

The breakthrough company takes 10-20 years to reach durable scale. The founder who burns out at year 4, or who treats the company as a stage in a portfolio of life experiences, will not reach the destination regardless of how strong the first four traits are.

The diagnostic is harder, because stamina is forward-looking. Maples's proxies: what has the founder demonstrated stamina on in the past? What is the founder's relationship to setbacks? Does the founder treat the company as identity or as project?

Cross-reference [[Brad Feld]] on multi-decade rhythm and the texture of long-duration founder operation, and [[Ben Horowitz]] on The Struggle as the default state.

## Why All Five Are Required

The Maples claim, defended empirically across the Floodgate portfolio: each missing trait predicts a specific failure mode.

- Missing insight: the company executes well but on the wrong thesis. Becomes a feature, not a category.
- Missing authenticity: the company optimises for the wrong problem because the founder does not feel the real pain. Solves the legible problem rather than the actual one.
- Missing creativity: the company produces a competent copy of an existing solution. Cannot win against incumbents or peers.
- Missing persuasiveness: the company cannot recruit or raise at the level required. Starves of talent or capital.
- Missing stamina: the company runs out of founder time before reaching durable scale. Founder leaves, company drifts.

The traits are not substitutable. Strong execution does not compensate for missing insight. Strong vision does not compensate for missing stamina.

## The Seed Investor's Filter

The practical contribution of the essay: a five-question diagnostic for seed pitch meetings.

1. State your insight in one sentence. (Tests insight.)
2. Why is this the problem you are working on, not another problem? (Tests authenticity.)
3. Walk me through a design decision where you chose a non-obvious path. (Tests creativity.)
4. Who is the best person on your team, and why are they here rather than at FAANG? (Tests persuasiveness.)
5. What does this company look like in fifteen years, and what does your life look like then? (Tests stamina.)

Founders who answer all five with specific, defensible content are candidates for backing. Founders who answer with abstractions on any one of the five fail the rubric.

## Principles Cited From This Source

- **Backable founders combine insight, authenticity, creativity, persuasiveness, stamina.** All five required.
- **Authenticity means the founder lives the problem, not studies it.**
- **Persuasiveness is verified by who the founder recruits, not how the founder pitches.**
- **Stamina is the multi-decade horizon, and most filters miss it.**
- **Each missing trait predicts a specific failure mode; the traits are not substitutable.**

# Feld On Depression

Brad Feld's ongoing blog series on his own clinical depression, beginning with a 2013 post and continuing across a decade of follow-ups at feld.com. The single most influential public modeling of founder mental health by a sitting venture capitalist. Before this series, founder depression was a private conversation. After it, accelerator programs, CEO peer groups, and VC firms had to build it into their stack.

## The Breakthrough Post

In 2013, Feld wrote openly about being in the middle of a depressive episode while running Foundry Group and Techstars at full speed. The post broke a taboo. A successful VC, mid-career, naming the condition by name, in public, while still operating.

> I have been a depressive my entire adult life. I have been on antidepressants for over 20 years.

The contribution is not the diagnosis. It is the public modeling. Someone the founder community looked up to said the words out loud, named the medication, named the therapy, and kept operating.

## Why Founders Are at Elevated Risk

Feld's posts and his subsequent talks identify a recurring cluster of conditions that produce elevated rates of clinical depression in founders.

**Isolation.** The CEO cannot fully share the company's problems with the team (creates panic), the board (creates intervention), or the family (creates burden). The internal narrative happens in a room of one.

**Identity fusion with the company.** When the company is "you," company setbacks become personal failures. There is no separate "self" to retreat to when the work goes sideways.

**Financial precarity.** Even well-funded founders often have personal financial exposure (deferred salary, illiquid equity, guarantees) that produces baseline anxiety not visible from the outside.

**All-in commitment.** The 80-hour weeks, the constant context-switching, the chronic sleep deprivation, the inability to take a full day off. Each is individually survivable; stacked across years, they erode the substrate that mental health requires.

**Cultural script that hides it.** "Hustle culture" trains founders to perform certainty. Admitting weakness in front of investors, employees, or competitors is read as signal of failure. The script makes the problem worse by making it invisible.

## What Treatment Looks Like

Feld is explicit and operational about treatment, which is part of why the posts had the effect they did. Not philosophical. Concrete.

**Medication.** Antidepressants work for many people. Feld has been on them for over 20 years. The cultural framing of antidepressants as "weakness" or "crutch" is wrong; the framing of insulin for diabetes is the right analogy.

**Therapy.** Regular ongoing therapy, not crisis-only therapy. The relationship with a therapist is itself the intervention.

**Peer support.** Other founders who have lived through depression. CEO peer groups (YPO forums, Hampton, Reboot) that allow honest conversation. The structured peer container does work the family and the team cannot do.

**Time off.** When in a depressive episode, reduce the load. Push the off-the-grid weeks (see [[Feld Startup Life]]) harder. The instinct to "push through" makes episodes longer.

> If you're depressed, get help. Today. There is no benefit to white-knuckling it.

## The Cultural Shift This Prompted

Trace the founder mental-health programs that exist today (Reboot, Hampton, the founder-therapy practices that took off post-2015, the explicit mental-health programming inside Techstars and Y Combinator) and most of them link back to Feld's posts as either direct inspiration or social cover. Sam Altman's posts on the topic. Tony Hsieh's death and the conversations after. The post-pandemic founder mental-health wave. All of it sits on top of the precedent Feld set.

## Principles Cited From This Source

(Will be added to [[Operating Principles]] Founder Resilience section.)

- **Depression is common in founders.** Isolation, identity fusion, financial precarity, all-in commitment, and a hostile cultural script combine to produce elevated rates.
- **Depression is medical, not characterological.** Treatment (medication, therapy, peer support, time off) works.
- **Silence makes it worse.** The cultural script that hides depression compounds the condition.
- **Peer groups do work that family and team cannot.** The structured container of other founders who have been there is irreplaceable.
- **If you are depressed, get help today.** White-knuckling is not a strategy.

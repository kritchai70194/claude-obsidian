# Naval How to Get Rich

The 40-tweet thread posted by Naval Ravikant on May 31 2018, titled "How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)". The compressed canon of his operating philosophy. Reproduced verbatim on nav.al, in *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant*, and across the web. The single most-cited modern text on individual wealth creation through leverage and specific knowledge.

## Why This Source Matters

The thread is the seed text from which every other Naval source flowers. *The Almanack* (2020) is a longer treatment of the same ideas. Every podcast appearance returns to the same skeleton. The tweetstorm is the highest-density version.

For a technical CEO, this thread is the operational answer to "what should I be compounding outside the company". Naval's claim is that the durable edge of a founder is built across a whole career, not inside a single venture. The thread names exactly what edge looks like.

## The Core Sequence

The 40 tweets move through a logical structure:

1. **Seek wealth, not money or status.** Define the goal correctly first.
2. **Wealth creation is positive-sum; status games are zero-sum.**
3. **You cannot get rich renting out your time.** Cap on hourly is biological.
4. **Own equity. Equity in a business is how you get truly rich.**
5. **Find specific knowledge.** Cannot be trained; must be discovered.
6. **Embrace accountability. Take risks under your own name.**
7. **Apply leverage. Four kinds: labor, capital, code, media.** Code and media are the new permissionless leverage.
8. **Become the best in the world at what you do.**
9. **Play long-term games with long-term people.**
10. **Earn with your mind, not your time.**

## Direct Quotes

> Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep. Money is how we transfer time and wealth. Status is your place in the social hierarchy.

> You're not going to get rich renting out your time. You must own equity, a piece of a business, to gain your financial freedom.

> You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale.

> Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people.

> The internet has massively broadened the possible space of careers. Most people haven't figured this out yet.

> Play iterated games. All the returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships, or knowledge, come from compound interest.

> Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine curiosity and passion rather than whatever is hot right now.

> Specific knowledge is often highly technical or creative. It cannot be outsourced or automated.

> Embrace accountability, and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage.

> Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication (code and media).

> Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. You can create software and media that works for you while you sleep.

> An army of robots is freely available, it's just packed in data centers for heat and space efficiency. Use it.

> If you can't code, write books and blogs, record videos and podcasts.

> Working hard is important. But the right place and right thing matter even more.

## Five Cited Principles

(Will be added to [[Operating Principles]] Founder Resilience section.)

- **Seek wealth, not money or status.** Three different games, regularly confused. Wealth is positive-sum and compounding. Status is zero-sum and addictive.
- **You cannot get rich renting out your time.** Hourly wages cap at biology. Equity in a business is the only path to financial freedom.
- **Specific knowledge cannot be trained.** Found by pursuing genuine curiosity. Recognizable because it feels like play to you and looks like work to others.
- **Code and media are permissionless leverage.** Unlike capital and labor, you do not need anyone's approval. Scales infinitely. Zero marginal cost.
- **Take risks under your own name.** Society rewards named accountability with equity, responsibility, and leverage. Most people choose the salary because they cannot bear the named downside.

## Operating Implications for a Technical CEO

The thread is operational, not aspirational. Three concrete moves it implies:

1. **Audit your time against equity, not income.** If you are paid hourly or salaried only, you have no leverage. The job is to convert work into ownership.
2. **Identify your specific knowledge.** What do you do that feels like play to you and looks like work to others. That is the seed. Compound it for 10 years.
3. **Stack leverage in the right order.** Specific knowledge first. Then accountability under your own name. Then code and media as multipliers. Capital and labor come last; they are bought with the first three.

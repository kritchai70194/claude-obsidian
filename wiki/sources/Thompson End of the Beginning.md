# Thompson End of the Beginning

Thompson's 2020 essay arguing that cloud + mobile represent the culmination, not the launchpad, of major tech disruption. Strategic implication: the big platform players (Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Google) are now stable like GM/Ford/Chrysler in 1920, and the next wave of value creation comes from companies that take cloud+mobile as a given and transform society on top of them.

Selected as the applied modern essay because it sets the strategic frame for builders deciding what to build in the 2020s.

## The Core Thesis

> "The cloud meant applications and data could be accessed from anywhere; mobile made the I/O layer available anywhere. The combination of the two make computing continuous."

Before: computing was discrete (sessions, locations, devices). After: always-on, always-accessible. The endpoint of multi-decade evolution from batch through PCs through web through mobile.

The corollary: no obvious next major substrate change. Voice, AR, IoT are incremental layers, not replacements.

## The Auto Industry Analogy

> "By 1920, manufacturing consolidated to three players (GM, Ford, Chrysler), yet the car's societal impact accelerated afterward, not during the competitive formation phase."

Cars: competitive formation 1900-1920, societal transformation (suburbs, highways) 1950-1980, decades later.

Tech: platform consolidation 2005-2015 (Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Google, Facebook). Societal transformation (Uber 2009, Airbnb 2008, Stripe 2010, Notion 2016, OpenAI 2022) ongoing for decades.

## The Stable Incumbents

- **Amazon** (AWS + retail, cloud-aggregator hybrid)
- **Microsoft** (Azure + Office 365, enterprise platform)
- **Apple** (devices + App Store + services, consumer aggregator)
- **Google** (search + Android + cloud, information aggregator)
- **Facebook** (social aggregator)

Structural features of the technology landscape now, not disruptable in conventional sense. New startups build on top, not against.

## Implications for Next-Generation Startups

> "New ventures no longer challenge cloud/mobile infrastructure directly. Instead, they take their presence as an assumption, and seek to transform society in ways that were previously impossible."

Strategic frame for building today:
- Do not try to be a platform; the platforms are taken
- Do not try to disrupt the incumbents at their core; the moats are deep
- Build on top of cloud + mobile; take that substrate as given
- Find societal problems that cloud + mobile makes newly addressable
- Aim to be an aggregator in a vertical the incumbents have not entered yet

Vertical examples this frame predicts:
- Healthcare delivery (telehealth aggregators)
- Logistics (Uber, DoorDash patterns)
- Financial services (Stripe, Plaid, embedded finance)
- AI applications (use-case-level aggregators on top of OpenAI/Anthropic)

## Why This Source Matters

For someone trying to build a unicorn in 2025-2030, this is the strategic context-setter. It answers:
- "Should I build new cloud/AI infrastructure?" Probably not.
- "Should I take cloud+mobile as given and build on top?" Yes.
- "What opportunities does this substrate make newly addressable?" Look for societal transformations that cloud+mobile enables.
- "How do I think about the dominant players?" As stable substrate, not as targets.

## Principles Cited From This Source

- **Platform incumbents are stable.** Do not attack at their core; route around into industries they have not entered.

# Carmack on AGI Keen Technologies

Composite source synthesizing Carmack's public statements on AGI and Keen Technologies from late 2022 through 2023. Drawn from the Dallas Innovates exclusive Q&A, the Dallas Innovates "Holy Grail" feature, Startup Archive's quote distillation, and The Register's coverage. Fidelity flagged as extracts because no single primary transcript exists. The Carmack/Bosworth Twitter Spaces and Lex Fridman 309 are the upstream primary sources for many of these quotes.

## Why this exists as its own source page

Carmack on AGI is now a load-bearing public-record position. His framing of AGI as a small-team, tens-of-thousands-of-lines, single-workstation problem is a counter-thesis to the OpenAI/Anthropic/Google scale-monopoly view. The arguments deserve their own anchor because they recur across every subsequent Carmack public appearance and inform [[Keen Technologies]] as an organizational design.

## Core verbatim claims

### Code size

> "This is code that conceivably one individual could write, unlike writing a new web browser or operating system."

> "The code for artificial general intelligence is going to be tens of thousands of lines of code, not millions of lines of code."

### Number of insights remaining

> "My bet is there's less than six key insights that need to be made. Each one of them can probably be written on the back of an envelope."

### Timeline

> "There's a 50% chance that somewhere there will be signs of life of an AGI in 2030, and I've probably increased that slightly, maybe 55-60% now."

> "I've got it at like a 95% chance" by 2050.

> "We are six, seven, eight years out from something really big and important being publicly visible."

### On approachability

> "That's incredibly hubristic to say, but this won't be unapproachably hard."

> "It feels like we are within striking distance of all those things."

### On the field

> "Nobody has line of sight on a solution to this today, we feel there is not that much left to do."

### Research method

> "I sit here at my computer all the time, thinking up concepts, documenting them."

> "Creativity is usually associating a little bit of this with some of that, viewed through this perspective."

> "There are fundamental research questions that need to be answered, and we have internal projects and angles of attack."

### Existence proof

> "We have our existence proof of humanity. The brain is like 40 megabytes" (of compressed genomic information for the architecture).

> "A simple program exploited at massive scale" (the principle he sees behind current deep learning successes).

> "I'm a half-dozen more insights away" (from biological-agent equivalence).

### Keen positioning

> "I could have written a check for $20 million myself, but accepting investor money forces me to be more disciplined and determined."

> "Zero near-term business opportunities" (his explicit focus discipline).

> "I'm positioning myself as one of these random test points."

> "It might be this grand spectacular success. Or I find two super-clever things and partner up."

### Future textbooks line

> "Future textbooks on how to build AGIs will include a chapter on the work Keen and others will do."

## Named patterns

**AGI is a small-program problem, not a large-system problem.** Tens of thousands of lines, not millions. Implied: the bottleneck is conceptual clarity, not engineering scale.

**Less than six key insights.** A bounded-search reformulation of AGI. Each insight back-of-envelope. The cognitive frame: closer to "find the right mathematical statement" than "build the right factory."

**Existence proof from biology.** The brain runs on ~40MB of compressed genomic instructions. Therefore AGI cannot require more compressed architectural information than that. Whatever ML systems are doing with billions of parameters is downstream of the architecture, not the architecture itself.

**Simple program at massive scale.** Carmack's reading of why deep learning works: not the elaboration of the architecture, but the brute exposure of a simple primitive to enough data and compute. Same pattern he sees as next-step for AGI.

**Single workstation, random test point.** The personal-research framing: he is one of many possible bets in the field, working on a workstation, and his thesis is "small team can win because the problem is small in lines of code." This is intellectually consistent with [[Carmack Farewell Meta 2022]]: the largest org in the world failed at VR by friction-loss, so a small org may succeed at AGI by avoiding that friction.

**Investor money as discipline.** "I could have written the check myself but I took outside money so I would be forced to be disciplined." A self-imposed accountability structure. Same pattern as the public .plan files: external commitment as a debugger for one's own decision quality.

**Zero near-term business opportunities.** Strict scope discipline. Refusing the obvious adjacencies (LLM products, ML consulting) so the AGI work cannot be hollowed out by revenue obligations.

**Larval phase, then production.** Maps to the [[Carmack Meta Connect 2022 Unscripted]] line. Carmack's stated arc: ~2 years of immersive learning, then begin substantive research output. Counter to the move-fast default; pro-deep-grounding.

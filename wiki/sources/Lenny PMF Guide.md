# Lenny PMF Guide

Lenny's most-cited essay on product-market fit. Not an original framework but the most thorough synthesis of how the best operators define and detect PMF. Compiled from Marc Andreessen, Steve Blank, Sean Ellis, Michael Seibel, Elad Gil and others, organized into a workable diagnostic.

## Working Definition

> "[PMF is when] you've made a product people want, can find and keep people sustainably, and makes a profit delivering to people at scale."

Three dimensions: desirability, sustainability, profitability. Most "PMF" claims fail at least one.

## The Three Patterns of PMF

How PMF actually shows up:

1. **Sudden and significant pull.** Hockey-stick growth, viral spread (Airbnb 2010, Dropbox 2008). Rare.
2. **Gradual but compounding pull.** Steady week-over-week growth, retention flattening. The "boring" PMF (Stripe, Notion). Most common.
3. **Milestone that proves the model.** First $100K MRR, first cohort retaining at year 1, first deal closed without discount. Typical for B2B.

Implication: do not expect PMF to look like Airbnb. Most unicorns hit (2) or (3).

## Specific Signals of Pull

The strongest indicators (ranked):

- Customers complain loudly when your site goes down (they care)
- People keep using when broken (they need it)
- Customers ask to pay before you ask them to (cleanest binary)
- Inflection in organic growth and word of mouth
- Sales cycles shorten dramatically
- Inbound demand exceeds your capacity

## The Sean Ellis Survey

Single most-cited PMF survey:

> "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
> A. Very disappointed B. Somewhat disappointed C. Not disappointed D. N/A

**Rule: 40%+ "very disappointed" = PMF.**

Caveats: works best with clear use cases, survey active (not lapsed) users.

## Cohort Retention as PMF Diagnostic

> Cohort retention is the most reliable quantitative measure of PMF. A retention curve that flattens (instead of declining to zero) proves that some group of users keeps coming back.

The shape is the signal, not the day-1 number. By day 30-90, does it flatten or asymptote to zero? Benchmarks per [[Lenny Retention Benchmarks]]:
- Strong day-30: >= 25% consumer, >= 35% B2B

## Multiple Definitions, All Correct

Lenny's synthesis: different vantages produce different (correct) definitions.

- Marc Andreessen: "Product/market fit means being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market."
- Steve Blank: "Validated that you've solved a problem worth solving for a substantial market."
- Sean Ellis: "40% of your users say they would be 'very disappointed' if they couldn't use your product anymore."

Real PMF satisfies all three: market (Andreessen), problem (Blank), customer (Ellis).

## Pre-Product vs Post-Product Signals

**Pre-product (cheaper, weaker):**
- Founder has been the customer for years
- Existing manual solutions / paid ugly workarounds
- Customer interviews surface the same pain in the same words

**Post-product (stronger, requires shipping):**
- Retention curve shape
- Organic growth inflection
- Sean Ellis survey result
- Inbound revenue

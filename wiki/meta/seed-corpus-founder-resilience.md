# Seed Corpus, Founder Resilience Pole

Source roadmap for Phase 1 of the unicorn-expansion roadmap (`~/.claude/plans/second-brain-jazzy-ullman.md`). Adds the **Founder Resilience pole**, a fourth pole alongside founder-operator, engineer-planner, and unicorn-builder. Answers the question the existing three poles do not: *how do I keep operating when everything is on fire*.

Every unicorn founder passes through extended near-death psychological terrain. Horowitz documents what wartime CEO actually means. Naval names the unfair advantages (specific knowledge, leverage, judgment) that compound across decades. Feld writes openly about depression in founders and the operating model of the board relationship. Chesky has been promoted from a Lenny-cited voice (founder mode) to a standalone mentor because his "founder mode" thesis is the antidote to the professional-management orthodoxy that fails most unicorn founders at scale.

This pole is **stage-independent** and **domain-independent**. Applies pre-PMF, post-PMF, marketplace, consumer, B2B SaaS, AI-native. The frames here are how to survive long enough for the other three poles to matter.

## Mentors (4)

### Ben Horowitz, c-000050

Co-founder/General Partner, Andreessen Horowitz. Former CEO of Loudcloud / Opsware (sold to HP for $1.6B). The single most-cited voice on operating a company through near-death without losing it.

### Naval Ravikant, c-000051

Founder/former CEO AngelList. Angel investor in 200+ startups. Author of the "How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)" tweetstorm and the philosophical-operator canon on leverage, specific knowledge, judgment, and wealth creation.

### Brad Feld, c-000052

Co-founder Foundry Group + Techstars. Author of *Startup Boards*, *Venture Deals*, *Startup Life*, *Founder's Dilemmas*. The most-published operator on founder mental health, board dynamics, and the multi-decade rhythm of company building.

### Brian Chesky, c-000053

Co-founder/CEO Airbnb. Architect of "Founder Mode" as named operating posture. Already referenced via [[Lenny Chesky Founder Mode]] (Phase 0); this pole promotes him to standalone mentor with three additional sources.

## New Decision Frames Planned (2)

12. *"Am I in wartime or peacetime right now?"* (Horowitz). Wartime CEO operates under existential threat; peacetime CEO optimizes for growth and culture. Wrong mode at wrong time kills companies.
13. *"What is my unique leverage compounding here?"* (Naval). Specific knowledge × judgment × leverage. If none of the three is compounding, you are working a salary not building wealth.

## Operating Principles Section Planned

**Founder Resilience (NEW section, ~10 cited principles):**
- Wartime mode vs peacetime mode (Horowitz)
- The Struggle is the default state, not the exception (Horowitz)
- Firing executives is part of the job (Horowitz)
- Take care of the people, then the products, then the profits (Horowitz)
- Specific knowledge cannot be trained, only attracted (Naval)
- Leverage is the multiplier; specific knowledge is the input (Naval)
- Play long-term games with long-term people (Naval)
- Founders should not separate work and life into hard buckets (Feld)
- Depression is common in founders; treat it as a real problem (Feld)
- Founder Mode means staying in the details that matter, even at scale (Chesky)

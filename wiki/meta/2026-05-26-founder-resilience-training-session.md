## Why This Session

The three existing poles all assume the founder can keep operating. None speaks with deep frameworks to:

- Surviving the multi-year emotional gauntlet (founder psychology, depression, isolation)
- Knowing which operating mode you are in (wartime vs peacetime)
- Compounding unfair advantages across decades (Naval's leverage stack)
- Operating as a founder at scale without sliding into the manager-orthodoxy trap (Founder Mode)

The roadmap audit (also 2026-05-26) identified this as Phase 1, highest leverage, applies stage-independently and domain-independently. The asymmetric leverage shortcut named in the roadmap (Phases 1+2+3 together) starts here.

## Method Notes

### Parallel-agent dispatch worked

Existing sessions used sequential per-mentor phases. This session ran 4 agents in parallel with disjoint output paths. Wall-clock time roughly 4-5 min total (vs 20-30 min sequential). Quality at parity because each agent's brief was self-contained with explicit format pattern, address allocations, content frames, and style rules.

The parallel approach is appropriate when:
- Output paths are disjoint (different mentors = different files)
- Format pattern is well-established (existed from prior sessions)
- Content briefs can be specified upfront

It is inappropriate when:
- Tasks share files or require coordination
- Format is being established (first session)
- Content depends on cross-mentor synthesis (do those serially)

### Fidelity declarations were honest

Of 15 sources:
- 11 declared `extracts` (paragraph-level quotes from publicly verifiable sources: a16z blog posts, nav.al, navalmanack.com, Feld posts, widely-reported Chesky talks)
- 3 declared `structural-summary` (themes from interviews without verbatim transcripts: Naval JRE 1309, Chesky Founder Mode talk, Chesky Altman conversation)
- 1 declared `interpretive-summary` (Naval Specific Knowledge synthesizes across the tweetstorm, Almanack chapter, multiple interviews on the single concept)

This matches the unicorn-builder session's 11/12 extracts + 1/12 structural-summary fidelity profile.

### Cross-pole connections were dense

Founder Resilience pole connects heavily to existing poles:
- Horowitz wartime ↔ Bezos two-way doors (when to invest deliberation)
- Naval long-term games ↔ Bezos regret minimization (long-horizon over local)
- Naval specific knowledge ↔ Karpathy first principles (idiosyncratic depth)
- Chesky founder mode ↔ Patrick Collison Stripe culture (founder-led at scale)
- Chesky design ↔ Carmack architectural planning (the founder personally drives the cross-functional vision)

The pole is not isolated; it deepens the others.

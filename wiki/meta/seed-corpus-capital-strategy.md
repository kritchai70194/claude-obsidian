# Seed Corpus, Capital Strategy Pole

Source roadmap for Phase 3 of the unicorn-expansion roadmap. Adds the **Capital Strategy pole**, a sixth pole focused on the multi-stage capital game: how to raise, what stage demands what behavior, when to blitzscale vs starve, how to construct contrarian thesis, how to spot inflections.

Existing brain assumes capital is available; this pole names how to operate within and across capital cycles.

---

## Mentors (4)

### Reid Hoffman, c-000098

Co-founder LinkedIn (2003), Partner at Greylock (2009-2023). Author of *Blitzscaling* (2018), *The Alliance* (2014), *Masters of Scale* podcast. The canonical voice on scale-stage strategy: when capital efficiency is the wrong question and prioritizing speed over efficiency creates durable competitive advantage. PayPal Mafia, board member at Microsoft.

### Bill Gurley, c-000099

General Partner at Benchmark (1999-2020). Board director at Uber, OpenTable, GrubHub, Zillow. Author of *Above the Crowd* (1996-present). The canonical voice on marketplace economics, burn-rate discipline, capital cycles, and "the unicorn party". The contrarian voice on late-stage private markets.

### Peter Thiel, c-000100

Co-founder PayPal, co-founder Palantir, founder Founders Fund. First outside investor in Facebook. Author of *Zero to One* (2014, with Blake Masters), built from his Stanford CS183 startup class. The canonical voice on monopoly economics, secrets, contrarian thesis formation, and what makes a 0-to-1 company different from a 1-to-n company.

### Mike Maples Jr, c-000101

Co-founder Floodgate (2008). Seed investor in Twitter, Twitch, Lyft, Okta. Author of *Pattern Breakers* (2024) with Peter Ziebelski. The canonical voice on backable founders, inflections vs ideas, "living in the future" as a precondition for breakthrough startups.

## Operating Principles Section Planned

**Capital Strategy (NEW section, ~12 cited principles):**

- The Alliance = manager/employee compact based on transformative tours (Hoffman)

- Burn rate is the company killer in capital cycles (Gurley)
- Late-stage private capital removes optionality (Gurley)
- Monopoly is the goal, not competition (Thiel)
- Last mover advantage in defined markets (Thiel)
- Secrets exist; you need a thesis (Thiel)
- Inflections precede ideas; ideas without inflections are tar pits (Maples)
- Live in the future to see inflections others can't (Maples)
- Backable founders combine vision + execution + storytelling (Maples)
- Most "moats" cited at fundraising are not real moats (Gurley + Helmer cross-reference)

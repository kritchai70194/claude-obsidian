# Seed Corpus, Decision Protocols + Customer Discovery

Phase 8 (final) of the unicorn-expansion roadmap. Adds explicit decision-quality protocols (Duke) and customer-discovery tools (Blank, Fitzpatrick) that the existing brain implies but doesn't name. Final phase, completing the 8-pole expansion.

This phase is augmentation, not transformation. The brain already has implicit decision discipline (Bezos two-way doors, Munger inversion, Kahneman bias inventory) and implicit customer discovery (PG do-things-that-don't-scale, Lenny PMF interviews); Phase 8 makes the protocols explicit.

---

## Mentors (3)

### Annie Duke, c-000199

Former professional poker player (WSOP bracelet winner, $4M+ career earnings). Decision-strategy author. Author of *Thinking in Bets* (2018) and *Quit* (2022). Co-founder of How to Decide. The canonical voice on decision quality under uncertainty: separating decision from outcome ("resulting"), the decision journal methodology, when to quit, the role of probability in everyday choices.

### Steve Blank, c-000200

Serial entrepreneur (8 startups, 4 IPOs). Adjunct Professor at Stanford, Columbia, Berkeley, UCSF. Author of *The Four Steps to the Epiphany* (2005, the foundational lean-startup text), *The Startup Owner's Manual* (2012). The canonical voice on Customer Development methodology: "Get out of the building"; customer discovery before product; the pivot as planned action, not panic response.

### Rob Fitzpatrick, c-000201

Founder of Foundercentric (consulting). Author of *The Mom Test* (2013) and *The Workshop Survival Guide* (2019). The canonical voice on conducting customer interviews that produce signal, not noise. Specifically: how to ask questions that even your mother can't lie to (the question pattern, not the relationship).

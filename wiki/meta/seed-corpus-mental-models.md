# Seed Corpus, Mental Models Pole

Source roadmap for Phase 2 of the unicorn-expansion roadmap (`~/.claude/plans/second-brain-jazzy-ullman.md`). Adds the **Mental Models pole**, a fifth pole that cross-cuts every other pole and every domain.

A unicorn founder enters domains they don't have first-hand experience in. Pattern matching from a multidisciplinary latticework of mental models is the toolkit that converts inexperience into useful judgment. This pole names the toolkit and the four canonical voices behind it.

The four mentors here do not overlap with the existing 17. Munger is the latticework architect. Kahneman is the canonical voice on cognitive bias and System 1/2. Taleb is the canonical voice on uncertainty, optionality, and antifragility. Christensen is the canonical voice on disruption and Jobs to Be Done as a causal frame.

This pole answers question types like "what am I missing", "what's the base rate", "what does failure look like from the other side", "what should the customer's job actually be".

## Mentors (4)

### Charlie Munger, c-000073

Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway (1978-2023). Co-architect with Warren Buffett of Berkshire's value-investing philosophy. The canonical voice on multidisciplinary "latticework of mental models" thinking. Author of *Poor Charlie's Almanack* (compilation). 1995 Harvard speech on causes of human misjudgment is the foundational text on biases applied to investing and life.

### Daniel Kahneman, c-000074

Nobel laureate in Economics (2002) for prospect theory. Author of *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011) and *Noise* (2021, with Sibony and Sunstein). The canonical voice on cognitive biases, System 1/2 thinking, and how human judgment systematically deviates from rationality. Foundational for product, hiring, pricing, and any decision under uncertainty.

### Nassim Taleb, c-000075

Statistician, options trader, philosopher. Author of the *Incerto* series: *Fooled by Randomness*, *The Black Swan*, *Antifragile*, *Skin in the Game*, *The Bed of Procrustes*. The canonical voice on uncertainty, fat-tailed distributions, optionality, antifragility (systems that gain from disorder), and the centrality of skin in the game.

### Clayton Christensen, c-000076

Harvard Business School professor (until 2020 death). Author of *The Innovator's Dilemma* (1997), *The Innovator's Solution* (2003), *Competing Against Luck* (2016). Originator of the Disruption Theory and the Jobs to Be Done framework. The canonical voice on why incumbents lose and how to think about what customers actually buy (the "milkshake hires" framing).

## New Decision Frames Planned (3)

14. *"Am I inverting? What does the failure mode look like?"* (Munger). Forward thinking is biased; backward thinking from "what would we have to do to guarantee failure" exposes the real constraints.
15. *"What's the base rate for what I'm assuming?"* (Kahneman). System 1 anchors on the salient case. System 2 must override with the population statistic. Most strategic mistakes are base-rate mistakes.
16. *"Is this position antifragile to the unknown?"* (Taleb). Robust = survives known shocks. Antifragile = improves from known and unknown shocks. Optionality structures, not certainty bets.
